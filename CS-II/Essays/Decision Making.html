Robbie Hutchings

Allen High School STEAM Campus

Computer Science II

Mr. Ben-Yaakov

Intro


 Biases will most likely be a factor in most projects when working together. This can disrupt the work process, and can cause us to lean one way in the decision making process. My ISP team (Independent Study Project) may have some decision biases, namely complexity biases, confirmation biases, control biases, desire biases, and test biases.

Complexity


 This is the most likely bias to be in our work process. We have a big game to make, and many ideas to implement. For it to be complete, all of the team members need to learn Lua (coding language), and then start working on the project. Needing to learn a language, in addition to not starting until later in the school year can add up to not being able to add everything we need, and omitting some things. To avoid this, I will learn the language early, and start working on the project before the end of the year. My hope is that I can start the work process early, negating this entirely.

Confirmation


 A confimation bias is when someone looks for information that supports their own opinion. The opposite is also true, when someone ignores disconfirming information. This could appear in my ISP group when figuring out what features to add. If some of us are biased in this way, it could ruin it for others. I will try to avoid this by collaborating with others, so that they can add their own opinions.

Control


 Control biases are made when one thinks they have more control then they do. This can lead to stress when things go the wrong way, and can stop the work flow entirely. This means we need to be ready to respond to situations that we are not ready for. I will prepare for this by being flexible with my work plan, and trying to adjust when neccesary.
