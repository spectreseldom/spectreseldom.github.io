Optimal Sorting

How it worked

Partners: Jacob Fesseha, Soohan Cho

Our optimal sort sorts every word into categories that are all the letters of the alphebet. it then continues onward to split them up based on the second, and third letters and so on. Because of the small amount of passes, this sort worked quickly and efficiently.

Sorting The Sorts

To see how fast our sort really is, we have made other sorts and compared our optimal sort to them by testing how many miliseconds it takes to run. The other sorts we will be testing are BubbleSort, InsertionSort, and SelectionSort. We will be testing 6 different lengths of arrays for each sort, 1 word, 10, 100, 1000, 10000, and 100000 words, in alphabetical order, reverse order, and random order, each 5 times for a total of 360 tests. The file names for the sorts are:
10e0.txt (1 word)
10e1.txt (10 words)
10e2.txt (100 words)
10e3.txt (1,000 words)
10e4.txt (10,000 words)
10e5.txt (100,000 words)

The command we will be using to time and test this is:
time cat (file name) | swift main.swift

Conclusion


Bubble Sort:
This sort is fine for sorting ordered, but becomes exponentially worse as it goes on, for reverse-ordered, and for random.


Insertion Sort:
This sort also if good for sorting ordered, but it does a little better as it goes on to reverse-ordered and random. Sometimes, it does worse than bubble sort!


Selection Sort:
This sort did the worst for sorting ordered, however, it does the best of them all among reverse-ordered and random. This shows the sorting methods, and which are the best, as shown by the graphs.
